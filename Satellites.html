  • The Indian National Satellite System (INSAT) is a series of multipurpose geostationary satellites launched by ISRO to satisfy telecommunications, broadcasting, meteorology, and search and rescue operations. Commissioned in 1983, INSAT is the largest domestic communication system in the Indo-Pacific Region1. The Indian Regional Navigation Satellite System (IRNSS), also known as NavIC, is a group of 7 satellites that provides Global positioning system (GPS) service in India. It provides real-time positioning and timing services and covers India and a region extending 1500 km around it
